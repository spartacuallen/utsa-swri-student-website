Degree Requirements

The physics doctoral degree program requires the completion of 81 credit hours encompassing the core physics classes (Classical Mechanics, Electrodynamics, Quantum Mechanics, and Statistical Mechanics), advanced physics electives, and research hours. Students must also pass a comprehensive written exam, unless the student receives at least 2 A's and 2 B's in the core courses. To advance to candidacy, students must then pass an oral exam. Once a candidate, and requirements for credit hours and course work are satisfied, students must then pass a dissertation defense.
