  • The Microphysics of Lightning by Maher Al-Dayeh - 11/14/13

    Throughout the last decade, observations of energetic radiation associated with thunderstorms and lightning have been confirmed from both space-based and ground-level platforms. These measurements have improved our understanding of the microphysics of thunderstorm electrification and the lightning discharge process. In this talk, I will review the physics of lightning initiation in context of these observations and their implications. I will also report on a series of energetic radiation measurements from natural and triggered lightning experiments performed at the International Center for Lightning Research and Testing (ICLRT) at Camp Blanding, Florida. These observations, along with the advancements in computational modeling and high-speed video technology, continue to provide new insights into the mechanisms of thunderstorm charging and lightning processes, all of which remain poorly understood.

  • American Geophysical Union (AGU) Conference - Fall 2013

    Each year over 20,000 Earth and Space scientists, students, and educations gather in San Francisco for the AGU fall meeting. AGU is a place to present the latest research in areas spanning from climate change to the solar system. Students in the joint UTSA/SwRI program attend AGU and give talks or poster presentations on their research; some of which have been awarded the prestigious “outstanding student paper award.”
